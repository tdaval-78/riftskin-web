The recommended workflow with RIFTSKIN

On Mac, the experience is more stable when you always follow the same sequence. That avoids unnecessary mistakes and false diagnostics.

  1. Download and install RIFTSKIN from the official page.
  2. Import only compatible custom skins into your library.
  3. Prepare your champion and your skin in the app.
  4. Run the injection from RIFTSKIN.
  5. In game or in the lobby, keep the champion's base skin so the replacement displays correctly.

What RIFTSKIN does and does not do

  • RIFTSKIN does not provide pre-installed skins.
  • RIFTSKIN is used to import, organize, and inject your compatible custom skins.
  • Free mode covers the core workflow.
  • Premium mainly removes comfort friction for regular use.
  • The web account is for Premium, billing, and account management, not for making the app usable in free mode.

How to keep your skin library organized

The best way to keep usage clean is to avoid piling up unnecessary imports. A clear library saves time and reduces injection mistakes.

  • Import only the skins you actually use.
  • Center your selection around your main champions.
  • Delete duplicates and tests you no longer use.
  • If you are Premium, use favorites to speed up your daily workflow.

When free mode is already enough

Most common mistakes

  • Launching the game without injecting from RIFTSKIN first.
  • Selecting an official skin other than the champion's base skin.
  • Thinking a macOS block means the app is broken, when it just needs to be allowed in Privacy & Security.
  • Using a library that is too heavy or badly organized.
